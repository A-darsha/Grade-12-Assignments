Definition of Normalization

Normalization is a process in database design that organizes data to minimize redundancy and improve data integrity. The primary goal is to divide large tables into smaller, related tables and define relationships between them. This helps to eliminate data anomalies and ensures that the database remains efficient and flexible.

Normalization Forms

1. First Normal Form (1NF)

A table is in First Normal Form if it meets the following conditions:

  • All columns contain atomic (indivisible) values.
  • Each column contains values of a single type.
  • Each column must have a unique name.
  • The order in which data is stored does not matter.

Example:

StudentID StudentName Courses
1 John Doe Math, Science
2 Jane Smith English, History

To achieve 1NF, separate the courses into individual rows:

StudentID StudentName Course
1 John Doe Math
1 John Doe Science
2 Jane Smith English
2 Jane Smith History

2. Second Normal Form (2NF)

A table is in Second Normal Form if it is in 1NF and meets the following additional conditions:

  • All non-key attributes are fully functionally dependent on the primary key.
  • There are no partial dependencies of any column on the primary key.

Example of a table in 1NF but not in 2NF:

StudentID Course Instructor
1 Math Dr. Smith
1 Science Dr. Jones
2 English Dr. White
2 History Dr. Black

To achieve 2NF, separate the table into two:

StudentID Course
1 Math
1 Science
2 English
2 History

And:

Course Instructor
Math Dr. Smith
Science Dr. Jones
English Dr. White
History Dr. Black

3. Third Normal Form (3NF)

A table is in Third Normal Form if it is in 2NF and meets the following additional condition:

  • All attributes are functionally dependent only on the primary key and not on any other non-key attributes.

Example of a table in 2NF but not in 3NF:

StudentID Course Instructor InstructorEmail
1 Math Dr. Smith smith@example.com
1 Science Dr. Jones jones@example.com
2 English Dr. White white@example.com
2 History Dr. Black black@example.com

To achieve 3NF, separate the table into:

StudentID Course
1 Math
1 Science
2 English
2 History

And:

Instructor InstructorEmail
Dr. Smith smith@example.com
Dr. Jones jones@example.com
Dr. White white@example.com
Dr. Black black@example.com
